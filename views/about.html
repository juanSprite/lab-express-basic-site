About

First Years

Pedro Sanchez was born in Madrid in 1972. His father was an economist, his mother a lawyer and his brother is an orchestra director. He started studying Economics in the Complutense University in Madrid in 1990, graduating in 1995. He joined the Socialist Spanish Party (PSOE) in 1993.

Beginnings in Politics

In 2004, he became a council member in the municipality of Madrid. Between 2004 and 2009 he was one of the 320 members of the General Assembly of the Bank Caja Madrid. He married Maria Bego√±a and had two daughters. He was elected again in 2007 as a council member for the City of Madrid. From 2008 to 2013 he was a professor in Economic Structure and History of Economic Thought in the University Camilo Jose Cela.

Member of the Congress of Deputies

He became a member of the Congress in September, 2009. He run for President of the Community of Madrid in 2011, though he lost. He worked in the OCU and achieved his PhD in Economics in 2012. In January, 2013, he became a member of the Congress of Deputies again.

The road to Presidency

In 2014, he was one of the potential candidates for the primary elections to lead the Spanish Socialist Party (PSOE). Some months later, the leader of the PSOE announced his ressignation and new elections were called to choose the new leader of the party. He got more votes that the two other candidates, and was elected General Leader of the PSOE and the Leader of Opposition in the Congress of Deputies.

In the general elections of 2015, he lost against his political enemy, Mariano Rajoy. In 2017, he became again General Leader of the Socialist Party after the primary elections where Patxi Lopex and Susana Diaz lost. After sentence of the National Court of Justice for a big case of corruption againt the Partido Popular(PP), the PSOE proclaimed a motion of censure and the PP finished being the party in power.
